Revolutionary Technology

Advanced All Wheel Drive

The 959 featured Porsche's first computer-controlled all-wheel-drive system, capable of varying torque distribution between front and rear axles depending on driving conditions. This technology was years ahead of its time and became the foundation for modern AWD systems.

Aerodynamic Mastery

With a drag coefficient of just 0.31, the 959's shape was optimized for high-speed stability. The adjustable ride height system could lower the car at speed to improve aerodynamics, while special ducts cooled the massive brakes.

Lightweight Construction

Porsche used advanced materials like aluminum, Kevlar, and Nomex to keep weight down while maintaining structural rigidity. The body panels were made from aluminum and reinforced plastic composites, making the 959 both light and strong.

Twin-Turbo Power

The sequential twin-turbo system eliminated turbo lag by using a small turbo at low RPMs and a larger one at higher revs. This innovative setup delivered smooth, linear power throughout the rev range, a feat that was revolutionary in the 1980s.

The 959 Legacy

1981

Porsche begins secret development of the 959 under the "Gruppe B" project, aiming to create the most advanced rally and sports car of its time.

1983

Porsche unveils the Gruppe B prototype at the Frankfurt Motor Show, showing off all-wheel drive, twin-turbo technology, and advanced materials.

1986

The production version of the 959 debuts, priced at $225,000 — making it the most expensive production car of its era.

1987

The Porsche 959 sets a production car speed record of 317 km/h (197 mph), becoming the fastest street-legal car in the world.

Today

The Porsche 959 remains one of the most collectible and influential supercars, with pristine examples valued at over $2 million. Its technology continues to inspire modern Porsche models.
