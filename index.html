The 959 Legacy

1981

Porsche begins secret development of the 959 under the "Gruppe B" project, aiming to create the most advanced rally and sports car of its time.

1983

Porsche unveils the Gruppe B prototype at the Frankfurt Motor Show, showing off all-wheel drive, twin-turbo technology, and advanced materials.

1986

The production version of the 959 debuts, priced at $225,000 — making it the most expensive production car of its era.

1987

The Porsche 959 sets a production car speed record of 317 km/h (197 mph), becoming the fastest street-legal car in the world.

Today

The Porsche 959 remains one of the most collectible and influential supercars, with pristine examples valued at over $2 million. Its technology continues to inspire modern Porsche models.
